Supersonic Kinetic Energy Tracking

Project Overview

This project explores the use of Arduino-based hardware and React applications to detect and track residual kinetic energy from supersonic objects. By deploying sensor arrays along the Brazos River, the system triangulates data and employs AI algorithms to predict object trajectories and potential interception points.

Supersonic Jet Tracking

The SR-71 Blackbird concept involves triangulated sensors detecting air pressure and kinetic energy shifts. The data is processed with Kalman filters, enabling real-time tracking and mock interception scenarios.

Arduino-Based Sensor Units

The sensor units feature an Arduino Mega, BMP280 pressure sensors, ADXL345 accelerometers, and MAX9814 sound sensors. These components gather data in rural environments, enabling precise detection and analysis.

Drone Swarm Interception

Downstream, drones simulate interception based on trajectory predictions, showcasing how AI-powered systems can intercept supersonic objects at sonic speeds.
